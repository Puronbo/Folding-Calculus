## 1. The Core Proof: The Spacetime Interval

To prove time behaves at a 90-degree angle to space, physicists do not use traditional Euclidean geometry, but Minkowskian geometry.

In normal 3D space, the Pythagorean theorem dictates distance (s) as:

$$s^{2}=x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}$$

Because time (t) unfolds at a perfectly perpendicular, independent angle to all three spatial axes, we must integrate it using the constant that converts time into a spatial distance: the speed of light (c). This creates the Spacetime Interval ($\Delta s^2$), which remains invariant (unchanged) for all observers in the universe:

$$\Delta s^{2}=\Delta x^{2}+\Delta y^{2}+\Delta z^{2}-(c\Delta t)^{2}$$

**The 90-Degree Proof**: The inclusion of the coordinates without cross-terms (like $xy$ or $xt$) mathematically dictates that the axes are orthogonal.

**The Minus Sign**: The negative sign before the time component is the "fundamental signature." It proves that while time is structurally 90 degrees to space, it is *hyperbolically* orthogonal, acting as a constraint on causality and the speed of light.

## 2. Relating Your Idea to Fundamental Theories

To elevate this to a universal theorem, we must look at how this 90-degree unfolding drives the physical phenomena of our universe through three major frameworks:

### A. The Constant Speed of Total Motion

In relativity, every single object in the universe is constantly moving through 4D spacetime at exactly one speed: the speed of light ($c$).

- If you sit perfectly still in space, 100% of your motion is directed along the 90-degree temporal axis (you age at maximum speed).
- If you begin accelerating through space, you "tilt" your vector. Because your total speed must equal $c$, moving through space forces you to divert some momentum away from the time axis.

**The Result**: Time slows down for you (Time Dilation). Your 90-degree unfolding angle acts as a cosmic zero-sum game.

### B. Matter Warps the 90-Degree Grid (General Relativity)

Your 90-degree angle assumption is only perfectly true in empty space. According to Einstein's General Relativity, the presence of mass and energy warps the geometry of spacetime.

- Massive objects pull and bend the grid lines.
- This causes the 90-degree angle between space and time to distort.

We do not perceive this geometric bending as a shape; we perceive it as the force of gravity. Gravity is literally the manifestation of the time-axis tilting toward space.

### C. Imaginary Time and the No-Boundary Proposal

If you want to take your theory to the absolute origin of the universe, you look to quantum cosmology. Physicists Stephen Hawking and James Hartle introduced the concept of Imaginary Time.

By multiplying the time variable by the imaginary number $i$ ($\sqrt{-1}$), time mathematically rotates by another 90 degrees.

This secondary 90-degree rotation strips time of its directional arrow and turns it entirely into a fourth spatial dimension.

Under this theorem, at the moment of the Big Bang, time did not have a singular beginning point. Instead, it smoothed out like the rounded bottom of a cosmic bowl, blending seamlessly into space.
