# Social Systems: Creases in Collective Behavior

## 1. Opinion Dynamics and Phase Transitions

The spread of opinions in a population follows Ising-like dynamics with a
critical temperature:

- **Fold (J):** Polarization — opinions snap to one of two clusters (90° away
  from center). The social field J enforces alignment within groups.
- **Unfold (∫):** Consensus — the diffusive integration of diverse viewpoints
  into a shared norm.
- **Crease (Nⱼ ≠ 0):** The tipping point — where the opinion distribution
  becomes bimodal and the system is critically sensitive to new information.

**Evidence:** Empirical studies of political polarization (McCarty et al 2006)
show the characteristic cusp shape: as "sorting pressure" (a) increases and
"issue complexity" (b) decreases, the opinion distribution bifurcates from
unimodal to bimodal. The bifurcation set 8a³ = 27b² separates the regimes.

## 2. Language Change

Languages evolve through discrete sound shifts (the Great Vowel Shift,
Grimm's Law) that are 90° rotations in vowel space:

- **Fold (J):** A phoneme shifts to a new position (e.g., /iː/ → /aɪ/ in the
  Great Vowel Shift). This is a discontinuous jump in articulatory space.
- **Unfold (∫):** The gradual phonetic drift between shifts — the integral
  of articulatory variation over generations.
- **Crease (Nⱼ ≠ 0):** The threshold of phonemic merger — the point where
  two phonemes become indistinguishable. Sound change is regular because
  it follows the crease geometry.

**Evidence:** The Neogrammarian regularity hypothesis — sound change operates
without exception — is the integrability condition. Exceptions (dialect
borrowing, analogy) are Nⱼ ≠ 0: the crease where the regularity breaks down.

## 3. Historical Revolutions

- **Fold:** The revolutionary event — the rapid, discontinuous overthrow of
  a regime (the 90° fold in power structure).
- **Unfold:** Reconstruction — the slow process of rebuilding institutions.
- **Crease:** The pre-revolutionary crisis — the point where the ruling
  system's Nijenhuis tensor (the consistency of its authority) fails.

**Data:** Skocpol (1979) identifies three conditions for social revolution:
state crisis, elite division, and peasant mobilization. These are the
(a, b) control parameters of the cusp catastrophe in political space.

## 4. Network Cascades

Information cascades (viral posts, riots, fads) follow cusp dynamics:

- **Fold:** The cascade — rapid adoption that spreads through the network
  like a phase transition.
- **Unfold:** Saturation — slow decline after the peak.
- **Crease:** The percolation threshold — the minimum number of initial
  adopters needed for the cascade to propagate. Below this threshold,
  the cascade dies.

**Evidence:** Centola's (2010) experiment on health behavior diffusion shows
that wide bridges (not just strong ties) are needed to cross the crease.
The 90° structure appears in the threshold model of Granovetter (1978).
