# Cognition and Consciousness: The Crease in the Mind

## 1. Attention as a Crease

Attention is a discrete selection mechanism in a continuous sensory field:

- **Fold (J):** Focus — the attentional bottleneck selects a subset of
  sensory input for detailed processing. This is a 90° fold from
  parallel (unconscious) to serial (conscious) processing.
- **Unfold (∫):** Integration — the attended information is bound into
  a coherent percept. The integral of features over the attentional
  window.
- **Crease (Nⱼ ≠ 0):** The point of attentional switch — where the
  focus shifts from one object to another. This is the crease in the
  attention field.

**Evidence:** Attentional blink (Raymond et al 1992) — when two targets
are presented within ~200–500 ms, the second is often missed. This is
the refractory period of the attentional crease: the system must
"unfold" before it can "fold" again.

## 2. Decision Making as a Cusp Catastrophe

The process of committing to a decision follows cusp dynamics:

- **Fold (J):** The decision — a discrete choice between alternatives
  (the 90° jump from deliberation to action).
- **Unfold (∫):** The deliberation — evidence accumulation over time
  (the integral of sensory evidence).
- **Crease (Nⱼ ≠ 0):** The decision threshold — where accumulated
  evidence reaches the boundary and the system commits. The Nijenhuis
  tensor of the evidence integration process is non-zero at the
  boundary.

**Evidence:** The drift-diffusion model (Ratcliff 1978) explains
reaction time distributions with a decision threshold that is exactly
the crease. The model is a cusp catastrophe in the evidence-accumulation
phase space.

## 3. Conscious Access as a Phase Transition

Dehaene et al (2006) propose that conscious access is a global ignition
phenomenon — a phase transition in the workspace:

- **Fold:** Global ignition — a local neural assembly becomes globally
  coherent (the fold from unconscious to conscious).
- **Unfold:** Sustained firing — the integrated activity across the
  workspace.
- **Crease:** The ignition threshold — where local activity exceeds a
  critical level and the global workspace ignites. Below threshold,
  the stimulus remains unconscious (subliminal).

**Data:** fMRI/EEG studies show a non-linear ignition at the threshold
of conscious perception (the P3b wave at ~300 ms). This is the crease
in neural space.

## 4. Memory Recall

- **Fold:** Retrieval — the reconstruction of a stored pattern from
  partial cues (the fold from latent to active memory).
- **Unfold:** Consolidation — the integration of new information into
  long-term storage.
- **Crease:** The encoding threshold — where short-term memory is
  committed to long-term storage. The Hebbian learning rule is a
  90° update (weight change proportional to pre × post activity).

## 5. Cognitive Dissonance

Festinger's cognitive dissonance theory:

- **Fold:** Belief update — the discontinuous change in belief when
  inconsistency becomes intolerable (the 90° fold in belief space).
- **Unfold:** Rationalization — the post-hoc integration of the new
  belief into a consistent narrative.
- **Crease:** The dissonance threshold — the point of maximum
  inconsistency where the system must change.
