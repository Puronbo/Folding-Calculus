# Music and Art: The 90° in Harmonic Space

## 1. The Octave as a 90° Rotation

The octave ratio 2:1 is the most fundamental interval in music. In
log-frequency space (the natural coordinate system for pitch perception):

- An octave spans log₂(2) = 1 unit.
- A fifth spans log₂(3/2) ≈ 0.585 units.
- A fourth spans log₂(4/3) ≈ 0.415 units.

The octave has a special property: it is a 90° rotation in the
just intonation lattice. The ratio 2:1 is the simplest ratio after the
unison (1:1), and it corresponds to multiplying frequency by exactly 2,
which is a translation by 1 in log space. Pythagorean tuning builds the
entire circle of fifths from 90° rotations of the octave.

**The 90° crease structure:**
- **Fold (J):** Octave equivalence — the perceptual identification of notes
  separated by a 2:1 frequency ratio. This is a 90° identification in the
  pitch helix (Shepard 1964).
- **Unfold (∫):** Melodic resolution — the integral of a harmonic progression
  toward a tonic. Cadence is the unfolding of harmonic tension.
- **Crease (Nⱼ ≠ 0):** Dissonance — where the harmonic lattice fails to be
  integrable. Dissonant intervals (e.g., the tritone, ratio 45:32) cannot
  be consistently embedded in a single key. The tritone is the crease in
  the circle of fifths.

**Evidence:** Shepard's (1964) tone illusion demonstrates the 90° structure
of pitch perception. The circle of fifths is a 12-fold 90° rotation
structure (each 90° step = 3 fifths = an octave + a fifth).

## 2. Harmonic Progression as a Cusp Catastrophe

- **Fold:** The dominant chord — V⁷ creates maximum tension (the fold toward
  the tonic).
- **Unfold:** The tonic resolution — I chord releases tension. The integral
  of the harmonic motion over the measure.
- **Crease:** The tritone — the interval (diminished fifth / augmented fourth)
  that divides the octave in half. In tonal music, the tritone must resolve
  (expand or contract by a semitone in opposite directions). This resolution
  is the crease crossing.

**Data:** The tritone was called "diabolus in musica" in medieval music theory.
It was the forbidden interval — the crease that cannot be integrated into
the diatonic system without resolution.

## 3. Rhythm and Phase

- **Fold:** Syncopation — the accent on an off-beat (a 90° shift in the
  rhythmic grid).
- **Unfold:** The downbeat — metric resolution.
- **Crease:** The "swing ratio" — the threshold between straight and swung
  eighth notes (typically around 2:1 = 90° in the ratio space).

## 4. Visual Art: The Golden Angle

- The golden angle (137.5°) ≈ 3π/4 = 135° (close to 90°). Phyllotaxis
  (leaf arrangement) uses the golden angle to optimize light exposure.
- The 90° is fundamental in perspective — orthogonal axes define the
  vanishing point system.
