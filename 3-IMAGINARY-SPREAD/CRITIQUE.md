# CRITIQUE — The Problems the No-Boundary Crease Still Faces

**Author**: Michael Grafiel Sayson Puno

---

## Honesty as Method

The crease framework makes bold claims. It should face the strongest
possible objections. This document catalogues the known problems and
open questions.

---

## Problem 1: The Perturbation Instability

**The issue**: When the Lorentzian path integral for the no-boundary
proposal is rigorously defined (Feldbrugge, Lehners, Turok 2017), the
amplitude for cosmological perturbations is an inverse Gaussian:
$\exp(+c \cdot \phi_1^2 / \hbar)$. Large fluctuations are favored over
small ones — the opposite of what we observe.

**Why it matters**: The no-boundary proposal predicts a wildly
inhomogeneous universe. We observe a nearly homogeneous one.

**Possible responses**: Modified boundary conditions (Di Tucci, Lehners
2019) or loop quantum cosmology (Bojowald & Brahma 2018) may resolve
this. The debate is active.

**Status**: Unresolved.

---

## Problem 2: The Empty Universe

**The issue**: The no-boundary wave function predicts a probability
distribution for the inflaton field that is strongly peaked at the
minimum of the potential — the state with the smallest possible
cosmological constant. This gives an empty de Sitter universe a
probability ∼$\exp(10^{120})$ higher than our observed universe
(Maldacena 2024).

**Why it matters**: The universe we see is exponentially unlikely under
the no-boundary proposal.

**Possible responses**: The Hartle-Hawking state may not be normalizable
for realistic potentials. We may not be in it.

**Status**: Unresolved.

---

## Problem 3: The Predictive Emptiness

**The issue**: A very recent analysis (Abdalla et al. 2026) shows that
when the normalization of the Hartle-Hawking state is computed
consistently, all probabilities are nearly 1. The proposal does not
discriminate between different possible observations.

**Why it matters**: A theory that predicts everything predicts nothing.

**Possible responses**: Too recent to have been digested by the
community. May fundamentally undermine 40+ years of work.

**Status**: Critical. Pending community assessment.

---

## Problem 4: The Definition of the Path Integral

**The issue**: The gravitational path integral is not rigorously defined.
The Euclidean gravitational action is unbounded below. The integration
contour over metrics is ambiguous.

**Why it matters**: Different choices of contour produce different
predictions. The theory is not uniquely specified.

**Possible responses**: Picard-Lefschetz theory selects the correct
contour (Feldbrugge et al.), but this produces the instability problem
(Problem 1).

**Status**: Active research area.

---

## Problem 5: The Arrow of Time

**The issue**: The no-boundary state is time-reversal symmetric — it
does not contain an arrow of time. Our universe clearly does.

**Why it matters**: The arrow of time must emerge from the theory, not
be imposed.

**Possible responses**: Decoherence within the consistent histories
framework may explain the emergence of a preferred direction.

**Status**: Plausible but not demonstrated.

---

## Problem 6: The "Sheet" Is Unknown

**The issue**: The crease framework posits a "sheet" with "contents"
that collide at the crease — but it has not identified what the sheet
actually is. Is it the quantum vacuum? The Higgs field? Spacetime
itself? Something else entirely?

**Why it matters**: Without identifying the sheet, the framework is
descriptive, not predictive.

**Possible responses**: The sheet may be the quantum fields of the
Standard Model. Or it may be something more fundamental — a pre-spacetime
structure from which both fields and geometry emerge.

**Status**: Open question.

---

## Problem 7: The Zipper Teeth Are Undefined

**The issue**: The "zipper teeth" model provides a vivid visual
metaphor but no mathematical formulation. What are the teeth? What
equations govern their engagement and disengagement?

**Why it matters**: A metaphor is not a theory.

**Possible responses**: The teeth may correspond to specific degrees of
freedom in the Standard Model — electric charge, color charge, spin.
The engagement may correspond to gauge interactions mediated by
bosons.

**Status**: Requires mathematical development.

---

## Problem 8: The Crease Density Generalization

**The issue**: Crease density is precisely defined and verified for
ReLU neural networks (Layer 0). But its generalization to physical
systems is conjectural. What is the "crease density" of a chemical
reaction? Of a nuclear fusion event? Of the early universe?

**Why it matters**: If crease density is a universal diagnostic, it
should be measurable in physical systems, not just neural networks.

**Possible responses**: For chemical reactions, crease density might
correspond to the fraction of molecules in the transition state. For
cosmology, it might correspond to the fraction of the universe
undergoing a phase transition at any given time.

**Status**: Speculative.

---

## What This Means

The crease framework is not a finished theory. It is a **research
program** — a way of looking at physics that suggests new questions
and new connections. The critique above is not a refutation of the
program but a map of the work that remains.

Every theory that eventually succeeded started as a premature synthesis.
The crease framework may fail — most frameworks do. But the questions
it raises, and the connections it reveals, are real.
