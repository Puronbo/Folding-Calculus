## The Problem of the Beginning

Classical general relativity breaks down at the Big Bang. Trace the
universe backward in time, and the scale factor a(t) goes to zero.
Density and curvature diverge. The equations produce a singularity —
a boundary where physics stops.

The Hawking-Hartle no-boundary proposal replaces this singularity with
a **smooth cap**. The universe does not begin at a point. Instead,
time rotates into imaginary time, and the geometry becomes a compact
four-dimensional space with no boundary — like the surface of a sphere,
but in four dimensions.

In the crease framework, this is not a mathematical trick. It is the
**ultimate spread** — the crease at maximum intensity, where the
temporal axis rotates fully into the spatial axes, and the fold that
separates space from time is undone.

## The Geometry of the No-Boundary

In the simplest case (positive cosmological constant, homogeneous
universe), the semiclassical geometry is:

1. A **Euclidean 4-sphere** of radius H⁻¹ = √(3/Λ)
2. Cut along an equatorial 3-sphere
3. A **Lorentzian de Sitter hyperboloid** attached to this slice

The point a = 0 (zero scale factor) is not a singularity. It is the
regular "South Pole" of the 4-sphere — a point like the North Pole on
Earth, where all lines of longitude converge but the geometry is
perfectly smooth.

**This is the spread in action.** Before the classical Big Bang, the
crease has spread so completely that the temporal dimension becomes
indistinguishable from the spatial ones. The fold that separates space
from time — the 90-degree orthogonality — has been undone by a second
90-degree rotation.

## The Two Folds of the Big Bang

The origin of the universe involves both folds:

### Fold 1: The separation of time from space (the first 90°)
The universe emerges from the Euclidean cap into the Lorentzian
expansion. This is the **first fold** — the moment when time acquires
its distinct character, orthogonal to space. The crease appears.

### Fold 2: The contraction of matter into structure (the second 90°)
As the universe expands and cools, the contents of the sheet —
initially a hot, uniform plasma — begin to differentiate. Particles
form. Atoms form. Stars and galaxies form. This is the **second fold**
— the folding of the sheet's contents into organized structure.

The Big Bang is both folds simultaneously: the folding of spacetime
itself (the metric) and the folding of matter within it (structure
formation). The crease that separates them is the initial singularity,
smoothed out by the spread.

## Inflation as Crease Propagation

The inflationary epoch of the early universe — a period of exponential
expansion driven by a scalar field — can be understood as the **crease
propagating through the sheet**.

The inflaton field rolls down its potential. As it does, the universe
expands by a factor of ∼10²⁶. Quantum fluctuations in the inflaton
become seeded as density perturbations — the "teeth" of the crease
imprinted on the primordial plasma.

These perturbations are the crease pattern of the universe. They
determine where galaxies form, where clusters gather, where voids
open. **The large-scale structure of the universe is the fossil of the
crease.**

## The Arrow of Time

If the no-boundary proposal is correct, the universe began in a state
of maximal symmetry — the Euclidean cap — where time had no arrow.
The second law of thermodynamics (entropy increases) emerged from the
first fold: the separation of time from space.

In the crease framework, the arrow of time is a **consequence of the
crease**. Before the fold, time and space are undifferentiated. The
fold creates a distinction between them, and that distinction creates
a direction — from the Euclidean cap (no time) to the Lorentzian
expansion (time with an arrow).

The arrow points away from the crease.

## The Ultimate Unfold

If the universe continues to expand forever (as current evidence
suggests), then the crease will eventually unfold completely. Matter
will decay. Black holes will evaporate. The universe will approach a
state of maximum entropy — thermal equilibrium at nearly zero
temperature.

This is the **ultimate unfold** — the complete relaxation of every fold
in the sheet, from the largest cosmic structures down to the smallest
particles.

The cycle, if one exists:

```
Euclidean cap (no crease)
    → First fold (time separates from space)
        → Structure folds form (matter, stars, galaxies)
            → Creases propagate (reactions, fusion, life)
        → Structure unfolds (black hole evaporation, decay)
    → Second fold relaxes (space and time merge)
Euclidean cap (no crease)
```

Whether this cycle repeats — whether the universe can refold after
unfolding completely — is the deepest open question.
