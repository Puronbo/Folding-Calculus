# The 90-Degree Complex Manifold: A Unified Geometric Framework

## Abstract

We propose a unified geometric framework in which the universe is
modeled as a 90-Degree Complex Manifold M of complex dimension n,
equipped with an almost complex structure J satisfying J² = −I. Physical
phenomena across all domains — from neural computation to cosmology —
are described as sections of sheaves ℱ_D over M, where each domain D
defines a sheaf of physical fields. The central object is the **crease**:
the locus where the Nijenhuis tensor Nⱼ fails to vanish. We identify the
crease density ρ(p) = ‖Nⱼ(p)‖ as a universal diagnostic, and the cusp
catastrophe V(x) = ¼x⁴ − ½ax² − bx as the local normal form of every
generic crease.

The framework rests on three pillars: (1) **complex geometry** —
J² = −I and the Newlander–Nirenberg integrability condition; (2)
**singularity theory** — the A₃ (cusp) singularity as the universal
unfolding of the fold; (3) **sheaf theory** — physical domains as sheaves
with stalk multiplicity at the crease.

Empirically, the framework originates from crease density in ReLU neural
networks, where ρ(p) measures the fraction of neurons at their switching
threshold (z ≈ 0). Five reproducible experiments demonstrate that crease
density tracks boundary complexity (r = +0.97), stabilizes before loss
convergence, detects out-of-distribution inputs (AUROC 0.884), and
identifies redundant neurons for pruning. We show that the universal
approximation theorem for ReLU networks is the geometric fold-and-cut
theorem in N dimensions.

Geometrically, the canonical almost complex structure J₀ on Minkowski
spacetime produces the 90° orthogonality between time and space,
identified with the spacetime interval and the light cone as the crease.
We extend the framework to chemistry (reaction fronts as creases),
nuclear physics (Coulomb barrier as crease), protein folding (hydrophobic
collapse as crease), phase transitions, and cosmology (Big Bang as the
primary crease).

The mathematical framework is exact; the claim that it provides the
correct unified description across all physical domains is speculative
and presented as a research program. Only the neural network experiments
(Layer 0) are independently verified.
