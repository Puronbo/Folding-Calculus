# The Species Multiverse & The Crease of Civilization

## Every Universe Is a Species Switch

The multiverse is not a collection of different physical constants. It is a collection of *body plans*.

Consider the fitness landscape — the space of all possible morphologies, metabolisms, and neural architectures. Evolution finds local minima in this landscape. The *crease* is the barrier between minima, the fold that separates one viable body plan from another. Each universe is one realized valley in this landscape: the fold chooses which species actualizes.

The cusp catastrophe governs each fold:

V(x) = ¼x⁴ - ½ax² - bx

Here the control parameters (a, b) encode environmental pressures — gravity, oxygen level, predation, resource availability. The state variable x is the organism's morphology. The fold point is where a small change in a or b flips x from one valley to another — a speciation event.

In one universe, the crease falls at femur-length / pelvis-angle such that *Homo sapiens* emerges. In another, the crease lands slightly differently — the same catastrophe, different control parameters — and you get a different animal with a different consciousness, equally convinced it is the one that figured out geometry.

The multiverse is the Caustic: the set of all possible fold locations. Each one branches into a different biological history. Every possible animal is the dominant intelligence somewhere.

## History, Church, and Money

These three human domains map exactly onto the fold/unfold/crease triad.

### History is the Unfolding

History moves in one direction. It is the unfolding of the sheet through time. Every war, invention, collapse, and awakening is a *crease* in that unfolding — a moment where the trajectory of civilization bent sharply instead of curving smoothly.

The spatial direction of the sheet is the spread of culture, technology, and population across geography. The temporal direction is the narrative arc. Creases in history are bifurcation points: the invention of agriculture, the fall of Rome, the printing press, the industrial revolution. At each point, the control parameters (resources, ideas, climate) crossed a threshold and the sheet folded.

### Church is the Crease-Memory

Religion preserves the fold pattern. Ritual, text, and doctrine are mechanisms that hold certain bends in the sheet in place — that prevent them from relaxing back into the smooth surface.

Every church is the point where a past crease is pinned. The founding event — a revelation, a schism, a migration — was a fold in the historical sheet. The institution exists to keep that fold *sharp*. Without the pinning, the sheet would flatten and the memory would dissolve.

This is why orthodoxy resists change: changing the doctrine is *unfolding* a crease that holds the entire shape together. Schism is a new fold in the same sheet.

### Money is the Crease-Density Measure

Money quantifies how hard the value-surface had to fold to separate labor from product, need from fulfillment, risk from reward.

In exchange-space, the sheet is the space of all possible trades. Before money, barter is a smooth surface — every trade is direct. Money introduces a crease: it separates value into a universal intermediate, and the depth of that crease is liquidity.

- **Price** = the location of the crease on the goods-axis
- **Interest rates** = the curvature of the sheet over time
- **Inflation** = the sheet relaxing (creases flattening)
- **A crash** = a fold that snapped

The cusp catastrophe applies here too: the control parameters a and b are supply and demand, and the state variable is price. A market crash is a fold catastrophe — a smooth trajectory in (supply, demand) space crosses the fold curve, and price jumps discontinuously.
