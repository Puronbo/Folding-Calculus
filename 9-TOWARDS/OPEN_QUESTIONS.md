**Author**: Michael Grafiel Sayson Puno

This document lists the open questions in the crease framework. Each one

## A. Foundational Questions

### A1. What is the sheet?
The framework posits a "sheet" with "contents" that collide at the
crease. But what exactly is the sheet? Candidates:
- The quantum fields of the Standard Model
- Spacetime itself — the metric plus matter
- A pre-geometric structure from which both fields and geometry emerge
- The wave function of the universe

### A2. What are the teeth?
The zipper teeth model is evocative but imprecise. Possible mappings:
- Electric charge (±e) as the fundamental tooth
- Color charge in QCD
- Spin (up/down) as a binary tooth
- The ReLU neuron as an information-theoretic tooth

### A3. What equations govern crease dynamics?
The framework has no equation of motion. A crease dynamics would need:
- A Lagrangian or Hamiltonian for the fold
- A conservation law for crease density
- A propagation equation for the crease through the sheet

## B. Specific Research Questions

### B1. Crease density in chemical reactions
If crease density is the fraction of systems at the transition state,
can it be measured spectroscopically? Does it follow the Arrhenius
equation? Does it peak at the isokinetic temperature?

### B2. Crease density in nuclear reactions
Can crease density be defined for the Coulomb barrier? Does the Gamow
window correspond to a crease density maximum? Can sub-barrier fusion
be understood as tunneling through a crease?

### B3. Crease density in phase transitions
Does crease density diverge at the critical point? Is the critical
exponent of crease density related to the correlation length exponent?
Can the Ising model be reformulated in crease language?

### B4. Crease density in protein folding
Does the fraction of residues in transition states along the folding
pathway behave like crease density? Is the soliton threshold a crease
density critical point?

### B5. The crease in quantum mechanics
If the derivative is a fold, is the momentum operator a fold operator?
Does the Heisenberg uncertainty principle express a constraint on
simultaneous folds? Is quantum measurement a crease — a non-differentiable
point in the evolution of the wave function?

### B6. The crease in quantum field theory
Is a Feynman diagram a fold pattern? Is renormalization an unfolding
operation — integrating out high-momentum modes to reveal large-scale
structure? Is the beta function a crease density?

### B7. The crease in general relativity
Is the event horizon of a black hole a crease? Is the singularity a
point where the fold has become infinitely tight? Can the Penrose
singularity theorem be interpreted as a statement about crease
formation?

## C. Cosmological Questions

### C1. Does the no-boundary proposal work?
The Hartle-Hawking proposal faces serious problems (see CRITIQUE.md).
Does the crease framework offer any resolution, or does it inherit
all of them?

### C2. Is the arrow of time a crease phenomenon?
If the Big Bang is the first fold, does the arrow of time point away
from the crease? Can the second law of thermodynamics be derived from
crease geometry?

### C3. What happens at the spread?
If the crease spreads at critical energy density, what happens after?
Does the universe cycle? Can the Euclidean cap be observed through
imprints on the cosmic microwave background?

## D. Mathematical Questions

### D1. The algebra of folds and unfolds
Is there an algebraic structure underlying folds and unfolds? A group,
a monoid, a category? The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus states that
fold (derivative) and unfold (integral) are inverses (up to a constant).
What is the full algebraic structure?

### D2. Crease density as a geometric invariant
For ReLU networks, crease density is a simple count of neurons at
z ≈ 0. Is there a geometric invariant that generalizes this to
arbitrary functions, manifolds, or physical systems?

### D3. The crease topology
Can the crease pattern of a function (or a physical system) be
classified by its topology? Are there crease analogs of the Gauss-Bonnet
theorem, the Poincaré-Hopf theorem, or Morse theory?

## E. Experimental Questions

### E1. Can crease density be measured in non-neural systems?
What experiments could measure crease density in a chemical reaction?
In a plasma? In a Bose-Einstein condensate? In a liquid crystal?

### E2. Is there a universal critical exponent for crease density?
If crease density is a universal diagnostic, it should have universal
scaling behavior near phase transitions. What is the exponent?

### E3. Can the spread be observed?
If the Wick rotation is physical at extreme energy densities, can its
signature be detected in heavy-ion collisions, neutron star mergers,
or the early universe?

## The Invitation

Every question above is an opportunity. Some are within reach of a
motivated graduate student. Others require decades of work. None have
been answered yet.

The crease framework is not a finished theory — it is a **map of
uncharted territory**. If any of these questions moves you, the repo
is open. The code runs. The literature is cited. The framework is
yours to test, extend, or refute.
