The Three Mathematical Pillars

The framework rests on three disciplines of modern mathematics. This is not metaphor — these are the formal definitions underlying every claim.

1. Complex Geometry

J² = −I. The universe is a 90-Degree Complex Manifold M — a space with an almost complex structure J that rotates tangent vectors by exactly 90°. When J can be globally integrated (Nⱼ = 0), the manifold is a genuine complex manifold. The crease is where Nⱼ ≠ 0.

2. Singularity Theory

V(x) = ¼x⁴ − ½ax² − bx. Every generic crease is locally equivalent to the cusp catastrophe (A₃ singularity). The bifurcation set {8a³ − 27b² = 0} is where two folds merge — the "critical crease." This is the normal form of every physical interaction.

3. Sheaf Theory

Physical domains are sheaves ℱ_D over M. Each domain D (chemistry, nuclear, biology, cosmology) assigns a set of sections ℱ_D(U) to each open set U of the base manifold. The crease is where the stalk is non-trivial — where sections collide and interact.

See 1-GEOMETRY/FOUNDATIONS.md for the complete formal development. For a gentler introduction, start with THE_UNFOLDING.md.

Start with a Piece of Paper

Take a piece of paper. Fold it.

That sharp line where the paper bends — that's a crease. Every crease is a 90-degree angle. The paper on one side meets the paper on the other side at exactly 90 degrees.

Now look around. Everything is made of these.

A light ray bending near the sun? A crease in spacetime. A protein collapsing into its functional shape? A crease in chemical space. A neuron switching on? A crease in the decision boundary. The Big Bang? A crease so large it unfolded the whole universe.

The 90-Degree Complex Manifold is the observation that every sharp transition in the universe — every bend, every switch, every birth and death — is the same 90-degree crease at a different scale.

The Universal Normal Form

V(x) = ¼x⁴ − ½ax² − bx
  • x — the state variable (where you are in the fold)
  • a — the bifurcation parameter (how folded the sheet is)
  • b — the asymmetry parameter (how tilted the sheet is)
  • V(x) — the potential (the shape of the fold)

Change a and b, and the fold changes its shape. But the type of fold is always the same — the cusp catastrophe. This is the normal form of every crease in the universe, from a phase transition to a nuclear reaction to the light cone in spacetime.

The Fold-and-Cut Theorem

The fold-and-cut theorem (Demaine–O'Rourke, 1999): any polytope in ℝⁿ can be realized as the intersection of a hyperplane with a finite sequence of fold reflections.

ReLU layers are fold reflections. The universal approximation theorem for ReLU networks is the fold-and-cut theorem in N dimensions.

Learning = placing the folds. Crease density measures how many folds are active at the decision boundary. The Nijenhuis tensor norm ‖Nⱼ‖ is the same quantity, measured in a ReLU network's parameter space.

This is not a coincidence. It is the same geometry at a different scale.
